There are many times in my life in which I've heard people saying "Your life is today", "live every day as if it was the last one", "You don't know if you'll be able to do it tomorrow", and other phrases of the sort.

However, have I actually done that?... Maybe a few times, maybe less than the ones I would like to admit. But what if I actually go deeper into the meaning of those words and start thinking about my life as one that is going to end at any moment (hopefully, in the upcoming 50-70 years)?

Sometimes we live in a bubble of endless routines and we either realize this when it's too late or when something groundbreaking happens in our life that makes us reflect on our life goals. Hopefully, we do not have to wait for another groundbreaking situation to happen and bring that thought back into our heads.

How to dare? I think that the best way of daring is following our gut and starting to do what we always dream about without waiting for more years to go by.

For me, daring starts today.
